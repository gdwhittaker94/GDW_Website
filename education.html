I am a self-taught web developer. It seems to be the done thing these days to do a bootcamp or even a university-backed course in order to learn web development. Although tempting, I ultimately decided not to do one of these courses when starting, and here's why.

When learning something new, I like to take notes ... lots of detailed, organised, colourful notes! This is a time and effort-intensive process.

My main concern with bootcamps and courses was the short time-frame they advertised - typically around 3/4 months. Before I properly started learning about web development I already had an inkling that there was going to be a lot to learn (little did I actually know!) , and that a lot of what I was going to learn was going be hard, complex, and quite different from anything I had learned before.

Therefore, I wanted to give myself the best chance of learning and understanding all this new information properly, give myself the time and freedom to explore new topics and concepts in-depth, and to of course make detailed, organised, colourful notes about it all! Not to be rushed from one day or one week to the next, going from topic to topic to topic without really giving myself time for the information to sink in.

So, that's what I've done! And I think it was worthwhile. Being self-taught is not an easy route, and I recognise it has it's disadvantages in comparison to cohort-based courses. However, I believe that although I haven't received the same formal education and training as those that have done such courses, this doesn't diminish in any way the quality of my education, and I believe my notes (which can viewed below) attest to that.
